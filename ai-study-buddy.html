StudyBuddy: An Adaptive Educational Robot

Abstract

This project explores how artificial intelligence and hardware (ESP32, OLED displays, Servos) can be used to create an adaptive educational robot that supports interactive learning for children. The StudyBuddy prototype uses the Freenode ESP32 microcontroller paired with dual OLED displays, servos, and a speaker to simulate expressive and physical feedback.

On the software side, the system uses Vosk for offline speech recognition and TinyLlama from Hugging Face for lightweight language processing, allowing the generation of AI-powered responses in real-time. The robot was designed to address gaps in current educational AI learning tools, which often lack hands-on experience, physical interaction, and adaptability for younger learners or those with special needs.

StudyBuddy was tested in different environments to evaluate its accuracy, performance, and engagement level. Results showed that it could successfully respond to queries and questions, with up to 88% recognition accuracy in quiet conditions. Although limited by hardware constraints (using a laptop with only around 16 GB of memory), the robot provided multimodal feedback through speech, animation, and movement, which are known to improve memory retention and focus.

Feedback highlighted the importance of being more descriptive on how the robot works and giving some user guidelines for students to know how to use and interact with the robot quickly. Also, try to make it more realistic, expressive, and mechanical. The findings support that combining physical embodiment with AI can make learning more inclusive, personalised, and enjoyable. Future improvements will focus on adding emotion recognition, more interactive features, and classroom-ready usability.

Project Goals:

  • Design an interactive robot that uses AI to personalize lessons in real-time
  • Enhance engagement through speech recognition, motion, and dynamic feedback
  • Bridge the gap between digital and hands-on learning experiences

1. Introduction

1.1 Background & Context

Education has relied on one-size-fits-all teaching methods for decades, failing to cater to many students' educational and social needs; however, artificial Intelligence (AI) is becoming a key aspect of our everyday society. It advances the academic sector and revolutionises children's learning, shifting from passive actions to more dynamic and personalised experiences. Artificial Intelligence is no longer confined to areas like research labs but is now on the brink of transforming classrooms across the globe.

1.2 Driving Forces

The rise in AI has been driven by rapid technological advancements, increasing digital accessibility, and a growing demand for personalisation, especially in learning. AI can help in multiple aspects, such as adapting to each student's needs, styles, and pace. Offering them the necessary feedback and help to improve in areas where they are struggling, as well as their educational outcomes.

Artificial Intelligence would also benefit children with disabilities, especially with applications and tools like text-to-speech, language translation, and personalised learning plans, enhancing equity and accessibility. However, with all these benefits, many challenges and corners remain, including:

  • Overreliance on technology: Fear of lacking social interaction may hinder social development.
  • Ethical Dilemmas: Data privacy and algorithmic bias require careful regulation.
  • Engagement limitations: Many AI tools are screen-based and lack physical interaction.

1.3 Current Applications

AI is already making a significant impact on digital learning:

  • Chatbots and tutors (e.g., Duolingo AI tutor) provide instant practice, feedback, and conversational learning.
  • Personalised learning plans (e.g., Khan Academy) that adapt to the student's progress.
  • Adaptive assessments identify students' knowledge gaps, recommending resources and help.

These platforms remain digitally confined despite these innovations, missing tangible, interactive learning opportunities. As childhood development thrives on tactile engagement, there is a massive gap in AI tools that combine physical interaction with digital intelligence.

1.4 Research Problem Gap

While AI enhances personalised learning and has significant potential in education, many of these tools:

  1. Are not always accessible, especially to children with learning disabilities.
  2. Do not continually update in real-time to meet individual learning needs.
  3. Lack physical interactivity, reducing engagement for students.

This project aims to bridge the digital and physical learning gap by developing an educational robot that adapts to the child's projects and enhances engagement through AI-driven interactions.

2. Methodology Overview

The StudyBuddy system was developed through the following approach:

  1. Hardware Selection: ESP32, servos, OLED displays, and a speaker
  2. AI Integration: Vosk for speech-to-text, Hugging Face's TinyLlama for adaptive response generation
  3. Prototype testing: Conducted in various environments to assess accuracy and performance

While broader user testing was outside the scope of this prototype phase, individual experimentation enabled the system to be evaluated for functionality, adaptability, and potential scalability.

3. System Design & Implementation

3.1 Hardware Architecture

StudyBuddy combines hardware components with software tools to create an interactive learning companion. The hardware design features a 3D-printed case with dual OLED displays for facial expressions and text, servo-driven arm movements, and audio feedback.

StudyBuddy Casing

3D-printed casing for the StudyBuddy robot with compartments for displays and components

Key Components

  • ESP32 Microcontroller: Core processing unit managing all interactions
  • OLED Displays (2): One for facial expressions, another for text/content display
  • Servo Motors: Enable physical movements and gestures
  • Speaker: Provides audio feedback and responses
  • Microphone (INMP441): Captures voice input for speech recognition

Hardware Challenge: While the initial design included the INMP441 microphone, testing revealed inconsistent performance. The solution involved using the computer's built-in microphone for the prototype, demonstrating the project's adaptability and problem-solving approach.

3.2 Software Architecture

The software pipeline consists of several key components working together:

  • Speech Recognition: Vosk for offline speech-to-text processing
    • Open-source solution that doesn't require internet connectivity
    • Chosen over Google Speech-to-Text to avoid credit-based costs
  • Natural Language Processing: TinyLlama from Hugging Face Transformers
    • Lightweight language model for generating responses
    • Balance between size and performance to enable real-time interaction
  • Knowledge Base: Wikipedia API integration
    • Provides factual information for educational responses
    • Open-source solution chosen after testing Khan Academy and OpenStack APIs
  • Multimodal Feedback:
    • Python for high-level logic (user input, AI response generation)
    • ESP32 for physical outputs (expressions, servo movements, sounds)

Software Challenge: AI Model Selection

Initial tests with larger language models (Llama 2, DeepSeek) showed promising response quality but were too slow for real-time interaction. TinyLlama was selected as the optimal balance between performance and resource requirements, though sacrificing some response coherence.

3.3 Adaptive Learning System

A key innovation in StudyBuddy is its ability to adapt to individual learners. The system tracks student performance and adjusts its approach using a lightweight Bayesian skill estimation method. 

# Key adaptive learning algorithm
def update_subject_skill(self, subject, topic, correct):
    # Current estimates
    current_skill = self.profile['skills'][subject][topic]
    
    # Bayesian update weights
    surprise_factor = 1.0
    if (correct and current_skill < 0.5) or (not correct and current_skill > 0.5):
        surprise_factor = 1.5  # Higher updates for surprising results
        
    # Update skill estimate
    if correct:
        new_skill = current_skill + (1 - current_skill) * 0.2 * surprise_factor
    else:
        new_skill = current_skill * (1 - 0.2 * surprise_factor)
        
    # Store updated skill
    self.profile['skills'][subject][topic] = new_skill

This approach enables:

  • Personalized difficulty scaling based on demonstrated skills
  • Targeted practice recommendations focusing on weak areas
  • Learning style adaptation (visual, auditory, kinesthetic)

4. User Interaction & Experience

4.1 How StudyBuddy Works

StudyBuddy offers an intuitive interaction experience that begins as soon as the system powers on. The interaction flow includes:

  1. Startup Sequence:
    • OLED displays animate a friendly greeting
    • Speaker plays a welcoming tone
    • System enters listening mode, ready for user commands
  2. Main Menu Access:
    • User can say "main menu" to see available modes
    • Alternatively, direct questions skip the menu for immediate answers
  3. Learning Modes:
    • Ask Any Question: General Q&A using TinyLlama for factual or open-ended responses
    • Vocabulary: Word practice with spelling and definition exercises
    • Science Facts: Educational content about scientific topics with quizzes
    • Math Mode: Adaptive math problems based on student skill level
    • Show Progress: Visual display of learning statistics and skill development

5. Results & Discussion

5.1 System Performance

Testing of the StudyBuddy prototype revealed the following performance metrics:

Metric Result Notes
Speech Recognition Accuracy 88% (quiet)
72% (noisy)		 Performance degraded significantly with background noise
Response Accuracy 85% TinyLlama outperformed T5, but occasional hallucinations occurred
Response Time 1-3 seconds Acceptable for maintaining natural conversation flow
Hardware Reliability 90% Servo attachment issues required hot glue solution
Key Finding: Physical Interaction Impact

The combination of visual expressions, sound effects, and physical movements significantly enhanced engagement compared to screen-only or voice-only interfaces. Even with basic servo gestures, users reported a stronger sense of connection with the learning system.

5.2 Comparison to Existing Solutions

When compared to commercial educational platforms like Duolingo and Khan Academy:

  • Advantages:
    • Real-time physical feedback lacking in screen-based tools
    • Open-ended conversation rather than structured multiple-choice
    • Adaptivity that dynamically responds to learner progress
    • Multimodal interaction supporting different learning styles
  • Limitations:
    • Less comprehensive content library compared to established platforms
    • Variable AI response quality, especially with smaller models
    • Hardware dependencies that increase system complexity

StudyBuddy offers a complementary approach to digital learning rather than a replacement for existing tools. Its greatest strength lies in providing tangible, physically engaging interactions for learners who benefit from hands-on experiences.

5.3 User Feedback

After demonstration and presentation, feedback highlighted several areas for improvement:

Key Feedback Points:

  • Realism: More mechanical, humanoid design would better simulate a tutor or companion
  • Instructions: Clearer guidance on how to interact with StudyBuddy is needed
  • Hands-on Teaching: Expand capabilities to guide physical tasks like building circuits
  • Interaction Modes: Add more engaging content like mini-games and storytelling

This feedback reinforces the value of combining technical performance with child-centered design principles and will inform future iterations of the system.

---

6. Conclusion & Future Work

6.1 Key Findings

This project demonstrated how low-cost hardware and open-source AI tools can be combined to create an interactive, engaging, and educational robot. The StudyBuddy prototype showed that:

  • AI-driven personalization significantly enhances student engagement by adapting in real-time
  • Physical interaction through gestures, expressions, and feedback improves focus and memory
  • Multimodal feedback makes learning more inclusive and accessible

6.2 Limitations

Despite promising outcomes, several limitations constrained the final implementation:

  • Hardware Limitations: ESP32's memory and processing capabilities required laptop support
  • Speech Recognition Challenges: Struggled in noisy environments with inconsistent performance
  • Knowledge Base Quality: Reliance on Wikipedia introduced potential for outdated or biased information
  • Human Nuance Detection: Limited ability to recognize non-verbal cues like confusion

6.3 Future Improvements

Future work will focus on enhancing the system in several key areas:

  1. Hardware Upgrades:
    • Integration of Raspberry Pi 5 for improved local processing
    • More mechanical body design with expanded movement capabilities
    • ESP32 camera module for visual recognition and interaction
  2. Software Enhancements:
    • Emotion recognition through facial and tonal analysis
    • More diverse lesson plans and adaptive content delivery
    • Cloud-AI hybrid approach for balancing performance and privacy
  3. Expanded Capabilities:
    • Multi-language support for diverse educational settings
    • Interactive games and narrative-based learning activities
    • Field testing in classroom environments with diverse learners

These improvements would transform StudyBuddy from a promising prototype into a robust educational tool that could effectively support diverse learning styles and needs in both home and classroom settings.
